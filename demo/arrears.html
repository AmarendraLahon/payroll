Arrears §08

Arrear = Σ over affected months of (recomputed amount − actually-paid amount). Worked example: DA revised 50% → 53%, effective 1-Jul, ordered in October.

DA Arrear — Mr. A. Das · Basic ₹25,500

Per-month DA diff = (53% − 50%) × ₹25,500 = ₹765. Higher DA also raises DA-on-TA: 3% × ₹3,600 = ₹108. Gross diff/month = ₹873.

Arrear trigger types

TriggerWhat changes
DA ArrearDA% raised with back-date (most common)
Promotion / MACP ArrearBasic re-fixed → DA/HRA/TA/NPS all differ
Pay Revision ArrearWhole matrix replaced (fitment factor)
Arrears reuse the ordinary salary engine pointed at month M with the revised rule — they are never a separate "arrear formula". Possible only because every rate is effective-dated (§17).
Cascade note: a back-dated promotion arrear is far larger — Basic itself differs each month, so DA, HRA, TA, NPS/GPF and tax all change, and schedule top-ups span every contribution. Arrears are taxable in the year of receipt (§89(1) relief via Form 10E, §15).
